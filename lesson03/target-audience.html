Target Audience


The audience for this site has a general scope, focused especially on those who have a business development process even from an early age, and all for the benefit of our region.



Personas


Persona 1

Emmanuel Guzmán, 24-year-old man. Future entrepreneur Installer of Telecommunications Networks. He is looking to start his own installation company for networks that provide rural service to people located outside the city.


Persona 2

Luz Janeth Vega, 51-year-old woman. Panamanian businesswoman looking for women who want to fulfill their dreams in a line of Aesthetic and Beauty Centers. She has been attracted by the great flow of aestheticism in the city for the last 2 years, and she plans to promote her line in Armenia, where she will establish her personal brand.



Scenarios


Scenario 1

Two teachers, Maria Elena Quiróz and Claudia Johanna Valencia, want to start their own teaching school for children, and need to know, through the local Chamber of Commerce, the requirements to establish their commercial registration and register their project. Part of your questions include the guide on the process to create a Labor and Administrative Company.


Scenario 2

Leonidas Osorio, an engineer with considerable experience in engineering, design and consultancy projects, has decided to create his company in the city and register it as General Manager. His objective begins by generating 18 jobs for a project that includes the construction of oil wells in the southern part of the country. He wants to know, through the page, what are the costs for his commercial registration and what scope it has in the development of the region, in addition to his benefits.


Scenario 3

3 young people, Miguel Ángel O. Sánchez, Samuel Antonio Carvajal, and Laura Camila Ortega, ages 23, 20, and 24 respectively, search through the Chamber of Commerce website for information on how they can be oriented to identify their potential as company with the Entrepreneur Advice program. They want to build their business as Designers for Children's Fashion, and they found out that through the Chamber of Commerce they can receive support and guidance so that their proposal becomes a successful company in this environment as demanding as fashion.


Scenario 4

A student of the professional career of International Business Administration seeks to support one of her degree projects with the specific information provided by the Chamber of Commerce in the areas of Legal, Financial and Tax Advice, and how these help the future entrepreneur. She knows that through the website she will find the lines of contact and the ways to filter the necessary information.


Scenario 5

A businesswoman who is simply browsing the web to find out a little about the costs of the Mercantile Registry and the "why" of these important documents in the creation of a company.
